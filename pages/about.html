About MGIT

Mahatma Gandhi Institute of Technology (MGIT) established by the Chaitanya Bharathi Educational Society (CBES) in 1997, is one of the premier Engineering Institutes in the self-financing category in Andhra Pradesh. The primary objective of this Educational Society is to create temples of knowledge so as to impart value-based education to the present and future generations of our country. The Institute with lush green campus, located in a serene and tranquil atmosphere near Osman Sagar lake, offers seven Under-Graduate Computer Science and Engineering, Information Technology, Electrical & Electronics Engineering, Electronics & Communication Engineering, Mechanical Engineering (Mechatronics), Metallurgy & Materials Engineering and Civil Engineering) and two Post-Graduate courses ( Mechatronics and Computer Networks & Information Security). It is affiliated to Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University, Hyderabad and is accredited by National Board of Accreditation, New Delhi. CSR-GHRDC rated MGIT as one of the Top Emerging Engineering Colleges of Excellence from A.P. at all India level.

About Mechatronics

Mechatronics is an emerging discipline which was started with the inception of MGIT in the year 1997, MGIT is the only college in the state and in the country to have started for the first time this innovative discipline of engineering, which is an integrated technology involving synergetic consideration and fusion of concepts of Mechanical Engineering, Electronics, Computer Science and Control Engineering and key element being the integration of these areas through design process. The department is accredited by National Board of Accreditation (NBA), New Delhi.

About Technovation 2k14

Technovation is an annual national level technical symposium conducted by the student association of Department of Mechatronics , under the guidance of eminent academicians, industrialists and budding technocrats of Mahatma Gandhi Institute of Technology, Hyderabad. This event is aimed at providing a platform for the engineering students to interact and share their ideas with their like minded equals. Mechatronics being a unique field that collaborates varied disciplines, allows students with various backgrounds like Electronics, Computer sciences and pure Mechanical to come together for creating innovative break-throughs in these technical areas. MGIT believes in formulating new conceptual events and competitions each year. In keeping up this motive, this year's Technovation imposes several new challenges in the field of robotic events, and other on-spot events.
