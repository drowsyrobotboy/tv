Mission:

Teams have to make a 'bot that can traverse through various terrains that come across its way (all-terrain track). Remember time is money, so make it sleek, stealthy and fast.

TEAM SPECIFICATIONS

A team may consist of a maximum of 3-4 participants.A team may comprise of any engineering student hailing from any college/institute or technological university (members from different colleges can form a team).
Entry fee: 250 INR for a 3 member team & 300 INR for a 4 member team.

DIMENSIONS AND FABRICATIONS

The 'bot should fit in a box of dimension 250mm*250mm(lxb) (no height restriction) at every given point of the event. The external device used to control the 'bot is not included in the size constraint.

ROBOT CONTROL REQUIRMENTS

1. The 'bot must be manually controlled (i.e. it can be controlled wirelessly or with wires. Off board power supplies are not allowed. All the power-supply must be onboard. Refer below for further details on battery and power.
2. If the 'bot is wired then the wire should remain slack under all circumstances during the competition. All the wires coming out of the 'bot should be stacked as a single unit. The wires should be properly insulated. Teams are suggested to use only rated wires. Loose connections or improper wiring may lead to direct disqualification even before the event.
3. If the 'bot is controlled wirelessly, the 'bot must at least have a four frequency remote control circuit or two dual control circuits which may be interchanged before the start of the race to avoid frequency interference with other teams. The case of any interference in the wireless systems will not be considered for rematch or results.
4. Remote control systems from toys might be used. Remote control systems available in the market may also be used.

BATTERY AND POWER

1. The 'bot can be powered electrically only. Use of an IC engine in any form is not allowed.
2. The electric voltage between 2 points anywhere in the 'bot should not be more than 12 V DC at any point of time. If a team is using AC voltage in any of its parts then the voltage should not exceed 12V AC at any point of time as well.

DISQUALIFICATION

a) Violation of 'bot specs.
b) Tampering the opponent's 'bot
c) Using the wires to pull or move the 'bot, i.e. in all cases the wire should remain slack, failure of which might lead to disqualification.
Note: In no case should the arena be damaged by any bot.

EVENT ROUNDS

1. The 'bot has to race through the all-terrain track. The one who finishes in least time shall be the winner.
2. Two chances will be given and average of best two times will be considered
3. There shall be few checkpoints. If a bot doesn't move/gets stuck then the team has an option of restarting from the previous checkpoint or they can skip the zone if they find it difficult to travel. But, they will be penalized for this and are restricted to use this option only twice during the entire race.
4. Arena will contain sand, gravel, slopes, pits, slippery surfaces, shallow water, rollers, etc.

PENALITIES

1. If the 'bot touches the walls/boundaries of the track the team shall be penalized (+3 sec)
2. Skipping or touching/restarting from checkpoint=>10 second penalty.
Note: In case of a tie, the 'bot with the minimum dimensions shall be declared the winner.

ARENA SPECIFICATION

Arena will be revealed on the date of event.

CERTIFICATE POLICY

1. Certificate of Merit will be given to all the winners.
2. Certificates of Participation will be given to all the teams.
3. The teams which get disqualified due to disobeying any of the competition rules will not be considered for the certificate.

DISCLAIMER

Judges' decision shall be treated as final and binding on all. Teams disobeying their word by arguing or creating a scene shall be disqualified..(The arena may vary slightly, but shall remain more or less the same, so all participant teams are advised to gear up for an all-terrain robo-race).
